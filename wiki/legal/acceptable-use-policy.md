# Acceptable Use Policy — Summary

This article is a plain-language summary of K-Perception's Acceptable Use Policy (AUP). It is provided for convenience and does not replace the binding legal document. Always refer to the official AUP at kperception.com/legal/aup [NEEDS-VERIFY URL] for the authoritative text.

[NEEDS-VERIFY — The specific clauses in the AUP are not confirmable from source code. The following reflects reasonable expectations for a zero-knowledge knowledge management product and must be verified against the actual AUP before publication.]

## Purpose of the AUP

The Acceptable Use Policy defines what users may and may not do when using K-Perception. It applies to all users on all plans (Local, Guardian, Vault, Lifetime, Team, Enterprise) and to all platforms (Windows desktop, Android, web).

## What is permitted

- Storing, organising, and sharing legitimate personal and professional notes, documents, and files.
- Using K-Perception for lawful business, academic, creative, or personal knowledge management.
- Collaborating with team members on shared workspace content.
- Using the API and CLI tools for legitimate automation of workspace operations.
- Exporting your own data in any supported format.

## What is prohibited

[NEEDS-VERIFY — specific prohibitions depend on the AUP text]

### Illegal content and activities

- Storing, transmitting, or sharing content that is illegal under applicable law.
- Using K-Perception to facilitate criminal activity, fraud, identity theft, or harassment.
- Violating intellectual property rights (copyright, trademark) of third parties.
- Processing or storing content that exploits minors in any way.

### Security and infrastructure abuse

- Attempting to gain unauthorised access to K-Perception systems, other users' accounts, or other users' encrypted data.
- Attempting to circumvent, reverse-engineer, or bypass the encryption or access control systems.
- Conducting denial-of-service attacks against K-Perception infrastructure.
- Probing, scanning, or testing the vulnerability of K-Perception systems without authorisation. (Responsible disclosure of genuine vulnerabilities is welcomed — see [Security Disclosure Policy](security-disclosure.md).)
- Abusing the API or CLI in ways that impose unreasonable load on the service (e.g., bulk automated operations that exceed rate limits).

### Account and identity abuse

- Creating accounts under false pretences or impersonating another person or organisation.
- Sharing account credentials with others outside of the intended multi-device use case.
- Creating multiple accounts to circumvent plan limits or suspensions.

### Spam and unwanted communications

- Using workspace channels or collaboration features to send unsolicited commercial communications.

## Zero-knowledge and the AUP

Because K-Perception is zero-knowledge — the server holds only ciphertext — K-Perception cannot proactively monitor content for AUP violations. K-Perception relies on reports from users and administrators to identify abuse.

This does not reduce your obligations under the AUP. Storing prohibited content in K-Perception violates the AUP regardless of whether K-Perception can detect it.

## Enforcement

Violations of the AUP may result in:

[NEEDS-VERIFY — specific enforcement actions depend on the Terms and AUP]

- A warning and request to remove the violating content.
- Suspension of the account or workspace.
- Permanent termination of the account.
- Reporting to law enforcement where required by law.

## Reporting abuse

If you believe another user or workspace is violating the AUP, contact K-Perception at abuse@kperception.com [NEEDS-VERIFY email]. Provide as much detail as possible without disclosing your own sensitive information.

## Changes to the AUP

K-Perception may update the AUP from time to time. Material changes will be communicated in advance. [NEEDS-VERIFY — confirm notification mechanism and notice period.]
