# Acceptable Use Policy — Summary

This article is a plain-language summary of K-Perception's Acceptable Use Policy (AUP). It is provided for convenience and does not replace the binding legal document. The authoritative AUP is published at [PLACEHOLDER — update before publishing: insert canonical URL, e.g. kperception.com/legal/aup].

## Purpose

The Acceptable Use Policy defines what users may and may not do when using K-Perception. It applies to all users on all plans (Local, Guardian, Vault, Lifetime, Team, Enterprise) and on all platforms (Windows desktop, Android, web).

## Permitted uses

- Storing, organising, and sharing legitimate personal and professional notes, documents, and files.
- Using K-Perception for lawful business, academic, creative, or personal knowledge management.
- Collaborating with team members on shared workspace content.
- Using the API and CLI tools for legitimate automation of workspace operations within documented rate limits.
- Exporting your own data in any supported format.

## Prohibited uses

### Illegal content and activity

- Storing, transmitting, or sharing content that is illegal under applicable law.
- Using K-Perception to facilitate criminal activity, fraud, identity theft, or harassment.
- Violating the intellectual property rights of third parties.
- Storing or transmitting content that sexually exploits minors in any way. This will result in immediate account termination and referral to law enforcement.

### Security and infrastructure abuse

- Attempting to gain unauthorised access to K-Perception systems, other users' accounts, or other users' encrypted data.
- Attempting to reverse-engineer, circumvent, or bypass encryption, key management, or access control systems.
- Conducting denial-of-service or distributed denial-of-service attacks against K-Perception infrastructure.
- Probing, scanning, or testing K-Perception systems for vulnerabilities without written authorisation. Responsible disclosure of genuine security vulnerabilities is welcomed — see [Security Disclosure Policy](security-disclosure.md).
- Abusing the API or CLI in ways that impose unreasonable load on the service, including exceeding documented rate limits through automated operations.

### Account and identity abuse

- Creating accounts under false pretences or impersonating another person or organisation.
- Sharing account credentials to allow access by persons beyond the intended account holder.
- Creating multiple accounts to circumvent plan limits, storage quotas, or account suspensions.

### Unsolicited communications

- Using workspace channels or collaboration features to send unsolicited commercial communications to other users.

## Zero-knowledge and the AUP

Because K-Perception is zero-knowledge — the server holds only ciphertext — K-Perception cannot proactively monitor content for AUP violations. K-Perception relies on reports from users and administrators to identify potential abuse.

This does not reduce your obligations under the AUP. Storing prohibited content in K-Perception violates the AUP regardless of whether K-Perception has the technical ability to detect it.

## Enforcement

Violations of the AUP may result in:

- A warning and request to remove the violating content or cease the violating behaviour.
- Suspension of the account or workspace pending investigation.
- Permanent termination of the account and all associated data.
- Reporting to law enforcement where required by law or where the violation involves content that endangers persons.

## Reporting abuse

To report a suspected AUP violation, contact K-Perception at [PLACEHOLDER — update before publishing: insert abuse reporting email, e.g. abuse@kperception.com]. Provide as much detail as possible. You are not required to disclose your own sensitive information.

## Changes to the AUP

K-Perception may update the AUP from time to time. Material changes will be communicated to account holders with reasonable advance notice. The effective date is shown on each published version of the document.
